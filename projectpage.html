Food Waste Structural Inequality

Project Description

This project addressed the dual challenge of food waste and food insecurity at NJIT and in the surrounding Newark community. It proposed a pilot program to repurpose surplus food from NJIT’s Gourmet Dining Services (GDS) to support the campus food pantry and local food banks—combining sustainability, community impact, and logistical innovation.

Key Contributions

  1. Research & Data Collection
    • Interviewed GDS management and pantry directors to gauge waste volumes.
    • Identified gaps in waste-tracking and resource allocation.
    • Analyzed NJ food-insecurity data to build the case for campus action.
  2. System Design & Logistics
    • Designed a container-based redistribution workflow for leftovers.
    • Split deliveries between campus pantry and local food banks.
    • Scheduled weekly distributions on high-traffic volunteer days.
