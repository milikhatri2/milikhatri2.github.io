Food Waste Structural Inequality

This project addressed the dual challenge of food waste and food insecurity at NJIT and in the surrounding Newark community. The initiative proposed a pilot program to repurpose surplus food from the NJIT Gourmet Dining Services (GDS) to support the campus food pantry and local food banks. The effort combined sustainability, community impact, and logistical innovation.

Key Contributions

  1. Research & Data Collection
    • Conducted interviews with GDS management and food pantry directors to understand scale of food waste.
    • Identified operational inefficiencies, including lack of waste-tracking tools.
    • Analyzed NJ food insecurity data to highlight need for campus action.
  2. System Design & Logistics
    • Created framework to redistribute GDS leftovers in disposable, single-use containers.
    • Proposed half deliveries to campus pantry and half to local food banks.
    • Scheduled weekly distribution on high-traffic days based on volunteer availability.
